# HR_Analytics
### This Repository has HR Analytics projects.


_Retain Your Best Before They Leave — Turning HR Data Into Workforce Intelligence._

## Project Overview
The HR Analytics Dashboard is an interactive business intelligence project developed to analyze workforce trends, employee attrition, job satisfaction, demographic distribution, and departmental performance. The main objective of this project is to help HR teams and management identify the major causes of employee turnover and improve employee retention strategies through data-driven decision-making.

This dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of employee data, including attrition rate, active employees, average salary, employee age distribution, education background, travel frequency, marital status, and job satisfaction ratings. Using interactive filters and visualizations, users can explore workforce patterns across multiple dimensions such as gender, department, job role, and education field.

The project was designed using Power BI for visualization and reporting, while analytical measures and KPIs were created using DAX and data transformation techniques. The dashboard enables HR professionals to quickly identify high-risk departments, dissatisfied employees, and workforce segments with higher attrition rates.

The analysis highlights critical HR challenges such as:
  - High attrition among Sales Representatives and Laboratory Technicians
  - Increased employee turnover in the Sales and R&D departments
  - Higher attrition among employees aged 30–36
  - Lower job satisfaction levels directly impacting retention
  - Frequent travel contributing to employee exits
This project demonstrates how HR analytics can support organizations in improving workforce planning, employee engagement, and organizational stability.


## Business Objectives
  - Monitor overall employee attrition and retention
  - Identify departments and job roles with high turnover
  - Analyze employee demographics and workforce distribution
  - Measure employee satisfaction across different job roles
  - Understand the relationship between salary, travel frequency, and attrition
  - Provide actionable recommendations to improve retention

# Key Insights
1) Employee Attrition is Significantly High
  - The organization has an overall attrition rate of 16.12%, indicating a noticeable employee retention challenge.
  - Out of 1,470 employees, 237 employees have left the company.


2) Sales Department Faces Maximum Attrition
  - The Sales Department contributes 56% of total attrition, making it the highest-risk department.
  - R&D contributes 39% attrition, while HR contributes only 5%.


3) Mid-Career Employees are Leaving More Frequently
  - Attrition is highest among employees aged 30–36 years.
  - Employees in this stage usually seek:
      - Career growth
      - Better compensation
      - Work-life balance
      - Leadership opportunities


4) Travel Frequency Impacts Retention
  - Employees who travel frequently show higher attrition compared to non-travel employees.


5) Job Satisfaction Directly Affects Attrition
  - Sales Representatives and Laboratory Technicians show lower satisfaction scores.
  - Employees with lower satisfaction ratings are more likely to leave.


6) Education Fields with Higher Attrition
  - Employees from Life Sciences and Medical fields represent the majority of attrition cases.



# Business Recommendations (Recommendations for Management)
1) Launch an Emergency Sales Department Retention Task Force
  - With 56% of all attrition originating from Sales, this department needs a **dedicated retention intervention program** immediately. Management should conduct structured stay interviews with current Sales staff, analyze exit interview data from former employees, and implement a targeted Sales retention package covering: competitive base pay review, commission structure transparency, clearer career path to Sales Manager/Executive, and reduced travel burden where possible.


2) Redesign Compensation Structure for Sales Representatives
  - At a 39.76% attrition rate, Sales Representatives are effectively a revolving door. The average monthly pay of $6,503 may be significantly below market rate for this role. Management should **conduct an immediate compensation benchmarking study** against industry peers and adjust the Sales Representative pay band. Consider introducing a retention bonus (e.g., 12-month anniversary bonus) and a structured performance-linked pay increase roadmap.


3) Build a Dedicated Mid-Career Development Program (Ages 26–35)
  - The 26–34 age group is the most at-risk for attrition. Management should create a **structured mid-career growth initiative** that includes: formal mentorship matching with senior staff, an internal leadership development track, bi-annual promotion review cycles (rather than annual), cross-departmental project exposure, and visible career pathing documents for each job family. Employees must see a future within the organization.


4) Create a Travel Reduction and Flexibility Policy
  - Since frequent travel correlates strongly with higher attrition, management should **audit which roles require frequent travel and why**. For roles where travel can be reduced, implement hybrid attendance policies, video-first client engagement models, and travel consolidation strategies. For roles that genuinely require frequent travel, introduce compensatory benefits — additional PTO, travel stipends, premium accommodation, and home office support.


5) Implement Job Satisfaction Improvement Programs for High-Attrition Roles
  - With 39% of the workforce reporting low satisfaction and **Sales Representatives and Lab Technicians showing the lowest scores**, management must address the root causes. These likely include: lack of recognition, limited autonomy, unclear expectations, and poor manager relationships. Implement **quarterly pulse surveys, role-specific recognition programs, and manager effectiveness training** for supervisors of high-attrition roles.


6) Invest in Career Growth Pathways for Life Sciences and Medical Graduates
  - Since Life Sciences (89) and Medical (63) graduates account for 64% of attrition, management must investigate whether roles filled by these graduates offer adequate career advancement. **Create clear, documented career ladders** for Lab Technicians and Research Scientists — including criteria for promotion to Senior Scientist, Research Director, and beyond. Consider partnering with universities for upskilling programs or tuition reimbursement for advanced degrees.


7) Improve Job Satisfaction
   Focus On:
      - Better workload distribution
      - Employee recognition
      - Learning opportunities
      - Managerial support


8) Benchmark Compensation
  - Average monthly salary should be benchmarked against industry standards to ensure competitive compensation.

## Conclusion
The HR Analytics Dashboard provides valuable insights into employee behavior, attrition patterns, and workforce performance. The analysis reveals that attrition is primarily driven by specific departments, job roles, mid-career employees, and lower job satisfaction levels. By implementing targeted retention strategies, improving employee engagement, reducing travel stress, and offering better career growth opportunities, organizations can significantly reduce employee turnover and improve workforce stability. This project demonstrates how data analytics and Power BI can help HR teams make informed, strategic, and proactive business decisions for long-term organizational success.
